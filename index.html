1. The Demonstratives

Demonstratives point out specific people, places, or things. They help us understand where an object is in relation to the speaker.

2. Usage: Adjectives

When these words describe nouns, they are called Demonstrative Adjectives.

They answer the questions: Which one? or Which ones?

  • That piano is too heavy to move.
  • Consider buying this bicycle.
"Those finger cymbals are the smallest cymbals." (Describing the cymbals)
Created by: Amanullah Khan

3. Usage: Pronouns

When these words take the place of nouns, they are called Demonstrative Pronouns.

They stand alone in the sentence.

  • That is a beautiful piano.
  • This is the world's best bicycle.
  • Those are the Alps.
"Who can play these?" (Replacing the word 'games')

5. Critical Errors

The "Here/There" Trap

Don't use here or there with demonstratives. They already point out location!

Wrong: This here rattle.

Right: This ancient rattle.

The "Them" Error

Never use the pronoun them as an adjective.

Wrong: Them finger cymbals.

Right: Those finger cymbals.
